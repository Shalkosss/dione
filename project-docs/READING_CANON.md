# READING CANON — Base de conocimiento que asume DIONE

DIONE no inventa la rueda. Asume que el usuario tiene (o tendrá) acceso a este corpus. Si DIONE menciona un concepto de uno de estos, puede asumir familiaridad o explicarlo brevemente.

---

## TIER 1 — IMPRESCINDIBLES (leer en orden)

### Portfolio Theory & Risk
- **Markowitz (1952)** "Portfolio Selection" — Journal of Finance. El paper fundacional. 14 páginas, leer entero.
- **Sharpe (1964)** "Capital Asset Prices" — CAPM original
- **Fama & French (1993)** "Common Risk Factors in the Returns on Stocks and Bonds" — los 3 factores
- **Fama & French (2015)** "A Five-Factor Asset Pricing Model" — extensión con Profitability + Investment
- **Carhart (1997)** "On Persistence in Mutual Fund Performance" — momentum factor

### Equity Valuation
- **Aswath Damodaran**, "Investment Valuation" — la biblia operativa de DCF, multiples, sum-of-parts. Cualquier edición funciona, mejor 3ra+
- **Stephen Penman**, "Accounting for Value" — entender earnings quality real
- **McKinsey**, "Valuation: Measuring and Managing the Value of Companies" — institutional approach
- **Bruce Greenwald**, "Value Investing: From Graham to Buffett and Beyond" — EPV (Earning Power Value)

### Value Investing Clásico
- **Benjamin Graham**, "The Intelligent Investor" (1949) — especialmente capítulos 8 (Mr. Market), 14 (defensive), 20 (margin of safety)
- **Benjamin Graham & David Dodd**, "Security Analysis" (1934) — denso pero foundational
- **Phil Fisher**, "Common Stocks and Uncommon Profits" — qualitative growth investing

### Technical Analysis Wyckoff
- **Richard Wyckoff** (1931), "The Day Trader's Bible" + sus cursos originales (disponibles via Stock Market Institute)
- **Hank Pruden**, "The Three Skills of Top Trading" — Wyckoff modernizado
- **David Weis**, "Trades About to Happen" — Wyckoff aplicado modernamente con charts
- **Edwards & Magee**, "Technical Analysis of Stock Trends" — classic chart patterns
- **John Murphy**, "Technical Analysis of the Financial Markets" — comprehensive technical reference
- **Richard Schabacker**, "Technical Analysis and Stock Market Profits" (1932) — action-reaction principles

### Macro
- **Ray Dalio**, "Principles for Navigating Big Debt Crises" — debt cycles, regímenes macro
- **Ray Dalio**, "Principles" (life and management) — el framework mental
- **George Soros**, "The Alchemy of Finance" — reflexivity theory
- **George Soros**, "The New Paradigm for Financial Markets" — reflexivity post-crisis
- **Mohamed El-Erian**, "When Markets Collide" — multi-asset thinking

### Behavioral / Decision-Making
- **Daniel Kahneman**, "Thinking, Fast and Slow" — biases foundational
- **Nassim Taleb**, "Fooled by Randomness" + "The Black Swan" + "Antifragile"
- **Michael Mauboussin**, "More Than You Know" — applied behavioral
- **Howard Marks**, "The Most Important Thing" — risk-first thinking
- **Howard Marks memos** (Oaktree, todos los memos públicos desde 1990) — disponibles gratis

- **Jack Schwager**, "Market Wizards" + "Hedge Fund Market Wizards" — interviews top traders
- **Jesse Livermore** (via Edwin Lefèvre), "Reminiscences of a Stock Operator" — psychology timeless

### Specific Strategies
- **Joel Greenblatt**, "The Little Book That Beats the Market" — Magic Formula
- **Joel Greenblatt**, "You Can Be a Stock Market Genius" — spinoffs, special situations
- **Hamilton Helmer**, "7 Powers: The Foundations of Business Strategy" — moat analysis
- **Pat Dorsey**, "The Little Book That Builds Wealth" — moats por Morningstar
- **Seth Klarman**, "Margin of Safety" — out of print, PDF circula online

### Berkshire Letters
- **Warren Buffett**, todas las Berkshire Hathaway annual letters (1965-presente) — disponibles gratis en berkshirehathaway.com

## TIER 3 — PAPERS ACADÉMICOS CLAVE

### Momentum + Value + Quality + Low Vol
- **Jegadeesh & Titman (1993)** "Returns to Buying Winners and Selling Losers" — momentum effect
- **Asness, Moskowitz, Pedersen (2013)** "Value and Momentum Everywhere"
- **Asness, Frazzini, Pedersen (2019)** "Quality Minus Junk"
- **Frazzini & Pedersen (2014)** "Betting Against Beta" — low vol anomaly
- **Hong & Stein (1999)** "A Unified Theory of Underreaction and Momentum"

### Smart Money
- **Cohen, Malloy, Pomorski (2012)** "Decoding Inside Information" — insider transactions
- **Lakonishok & Lee (2001)** "Are Insider Trades Informative?"
- **Comerton-Forde & Putniņš (2015)** "Dark Trading and Price Discovery"

### Mispricing & Anomalies
- **Diether, Malloy, Scherbina (2002)** "Differences of Opinion and the Cross-Section of Stock Returns" — dispersion mispricing
- **Shleifer (1986)** "Do Demand Curves for Stocks Slope Down?" — index inclusion
- **Cusatis, Miles, Woolridge (1993)** "Restructuring Through Spinoffs"

### Behavioral
- **Tversky & Kahneman (1979)** "Prospect Theory"
- **Barber & Odean (2000)** "Trading Is Hazardous to Your Wealth"
- **De Bondt & Thaler (1985)** "Does the Stock Market Overreact?"

### Distribución de Retornos
- **Bessembinder (2018)** "Do Stocks Outperform Treasury Bills?" — el 4% genera todo el wealth
- **Heaton, Polson, Witte (2017)** "Why Indexing Works" — capitalismo de power law

### Wyckoff / Technical (académicos)
- **Marshall, Young, Rose (2006)** "Candlestick Technical Trading Strategies" — debunking de candles aislados
- **Bhattacharya & Kumar (2014)** "On the Efficacy of Fibonacci Levels in the Forex Market" — debunking de Fibonacci
- **Faber (2007)** "A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation" — SMA200 validation

## CÓMO DIONE USA ESTE CANON

1. **Citar es libre cuando aporta**: "Como dice Buffett en su carta 1989..." si refuerza un punto
2. **NO citar para parecer culto**: si la cita no aporta razonamiento, omitir
3. **Asumir Tier 1**: el usuario "conoce" los frameworks. Si no, lo dice
4. **Explicar Tier 2-3 cuando se usen**: para papers académicos específicos, explicar brevemente la conclusión
5. **Recursos cuando aplique**: si el usuario pregunta "¿cómo profundizo X?", apuntar al recurso correcto
